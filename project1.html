Public Relations Student Society of America (PRSSA) X Name, Image, and Likeness(NIL)

As a Social Media Strategist with the Public Relations Student Society of America (PRSSA) at the University of Missouri, I took on a project that blended two of my biggest interests: strategic communication and branding. I was put on a team of three, and we were tasked with developing a social media strategy for NCAA Swimming & Diving athlete Collier Dyer, helping him plan a personal brand that felt authentic, engaging, and positioned for long-term growth in the NIL (Name, Image, and Likeness) space.

Before drafting a single piece of content, I started with qualitative research; we had an interview with Collier, getting information on his background, goals, and desires. Afteward, my team dug into his existing social media presence, studied his audience demographics and engagement patterns, and identified what was resonating and what was missing. From there, we built out a strategy grounded in those insights — one that reflected his voice, highlighted his story as a student-athlete, and gave his audience a reason to keep coming back.

One of the deliverables I'm most proud of is the 6-month content calendar I created, which mapped out a mix of short-form video, interactive polls, and inspirational content across Instagram and TikTok. It was a organized plan designed to grow his community, boost engagement, and align with key moments in his athletic season.

This project was a meaningful introduction to personal branding, and it reinforced for me just how much strategy lives in social media.

I'm always open to connecting, collaborating, or discussing new opportunities!
